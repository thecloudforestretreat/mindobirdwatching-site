Tubing in Mindo, Ecuador

Float the river on an inner tube in Mindo, Ecuador and choose between a Level I ride on the Mindo River or a stronger Level II descent on the Cinto River. Both routes combine cloud forest scenery, refreshing water, local transport, safety gear, and guide support for a playful river activity that works especially well for friends, families, and small groups.

What to expect

A chilled river activity with light adventure, plenty of smiles, and a refreshing way to enjoy the outdoors.

Relaxed but still fun

Level I tubing on the Mindo River covers about 3 kilometers and is the easiest introduction to the activity. The river gives the ride some variation, with calmer stretches broken up by small bumps and playful moments that keep it entertaining.

Great for groups

This is one of the better activities for couples, families, and friends because everyone can enjoy the route together. The Mindo River option is organized for groups, and the logistics include transport from the central plaza, helmet, life jacket, and guide support.

Cinto for more current

The Cinto River option is a Level II route with stronger current, a longer 6-kilometer descent, and two guides on the river. It is a better match for travelers who want the tubing day to feel more active.

Choose your tubing level

Two river options make it easier to match the experience to your time, energy, and appetite for adventure.

Level I 3 km

Mindo River

A 3-kilometer descent on the Mindo River, ideal for travelers who want a refreshing, easygoing Level I introduction to tubing without making the activity too intense.

  • Departure is from Mindo's central plaza.
  • Transport, helmet, life jacket, and guide support are included.
  • The route uses the Level I section of the Mindo River.
Level II 6 km

Cinto River

A 6-kilometer descent on the Cinto River, with stronger current and a total outing time of about 2 hours and 30 minutes from Mindo's central plaza.

  • The drive from the central plaza toward Cinto takes about 45 minutes.
  • Plan on roughly 1 hour descending the river and 30 minutes returning.
  • Transport, helmet, life jacket, and two guides are included because the Cinto River has stronger flow.

River feel and tubing highlights

The charm of tubing comes from how easy it is to enjoy while still feeling active, playful, and scenic.

Calm drifting and light rapids

The balance between slower water and little bursts of movement gives tubing its appeal. Guests get time to relax and take in the setting, but the occasional bumps keep the ride from feeling flat.

Refreshing outdoor fun

Water activities always bring a different kind of energy to a trip, and tubing stands out because it feels playful without being too intense. It is one of the easiest ways to cool off and have fun at the same time.

Good for mixed-age groups

Because the activity is less technical than some adventure sports, it often works well for groups with different ages and energy levels. That makes it especially useful for family trips and casual group outings.

Easy add-on to a bigger day

Tubing fits nicely into a day that already includes birding, chocolate, coffee, waterfalls, or a town walk. It adds movement and fun without requiring a full-day commitment.

Helpful notes

Come ready for cool water, relaxed fun, and one of the easiest group-friendly activities in Mindo.

Wear quick-dry clothes or a swimsuit, and bring a towel and a change of clothes for after the ride. Secure footwear helps around wet entry and exit points. A relaxed mindset makes the experience better, because tubing is at its best when you let the river set the pace and enjoy the ride.
