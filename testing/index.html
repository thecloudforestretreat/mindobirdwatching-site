Custom / Private Tour

Why Choose a Custom Private Birding Route?

Travelers searching for a private bird watching tour in Mindo often choose this option when they want target species, a slower personal pace, dedicated photography time, family comfort, or route flexibility beyond fixed schedules.

It is also the best format for serious birders who want to maximize time around hummingbirds, toucans, tanagers, Cock-of-the-Rock, and cloud forest specialties.

Popular Target Species in Mindo

Every custom tour is different, but many guests arrive with a short list of birds they hope to see. We use recent field activity, weather, season, access, and your experience level to build the most realistic route possible.

A

Antpittas, Cock-of-the-Rock, and Forest Icons

Depending on timing and local activity, a custom itinerary may focus on birds such as Giant Antpitta, the Andean Cock-of-the-Rock, Dark-backed Wood-Quail, Black Solitaire, and Long-wattled Umbrellabird. These are the kinds of species that often reward patience, early starts, and guide knowledge.

H

Hummingbirds and Colorful Cloud Forest Birds

Mindo is excellent for hummingbirds and colorful mixed-flock birds. We may plan time for Purple-chested Hummingbird, Little Woodstar, Tooth-billed Hummingbird, tanagers, barbets, trogons, flowerpiercers, and other cloud forest specialties.

C

Chocó Foothill Specialties

For guests interested in lower-elevation birding, the itinerary may extend toward productive Chocó foothill areas for species such as Chocó Black-throated Trogon, Blue-whiskered Tanager, Orange-fronted Barbet, White-faced Nunbird, and other regional specialties.

P

Photography and Rare Targets

Photography-focused tours can slow down around feeders, leks, fruiting trees, or active trails. Rare birds are never guaranteed, but a private route gives us the flexibility to adjust the day around current sightings and the best available opportunities.

Birding Locations We May Include

A custom tour lets us choose habitats instead of following a fixed route. Final locations depend on your target birds, road conditions, guide availability, timing, and how far you want to travel from Mindo.

M

Mindo Valley and Local Cloud Forest

Good for flexible birding close to town, hummingbirds, mixed flocks, tanagers, forest edges, and travelers who want a strong route without long transfers.

P

Paz de las Aves, Bellavista, and Tandayapa

Useful for antpittas, cloud forest specialties, dawn birding, and routes where patient observation can produce memorable encounters.

Ma

Mashpi Foothills and Chocó Slopes

Excellent when guests want a more ambitious route for Chocó specialties, foothill forest, colorful tanagers, barbets, trogons, and canopy activity.

S

Silanche and Lower-Elevation Extensions

Best for guests who are comfortable with a longer day and want to broaden the species mix beyond the immediate Mindo cloud forest.

FAQ Highlights

How early should we start?

Early morning is usually best. We will recommend a start time based on your target birds and season.

Can you tailor for photography?

Yes. Tell us your gear and style, and we will plan for pacing, light, and feeder time.

Can families or beginners book this?

Yes. Many guests choose private custom tours for a calmer pace, easier logistics, and personal guidance.

What birds can I see in Mindo, Ecuador?

Mindo can offer hummingbirds, toucans, tanagers, antpittas, trogons, barbets, flycatchers, woodcreepers, and the Andean Cock-of-the-Rock. The exact species depend on season, weather, habitat, and recent activity.

Can I request specific target species?

Yes. Send us your target list when you request the tour. We will review what is realistic for your dates and suggest the best route for your goals.

Which birding locations might be included?

Depending on your goals, we may consider the Mindo Valley, Bellavista, Tandayapa, Paz de las Aves, Mashpi foothills, Silanche, or other productive nearby areas.
