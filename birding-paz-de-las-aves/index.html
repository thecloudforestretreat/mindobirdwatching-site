Birds to Look For

Sightings are never guaranteed, but these are the types of birds that make the area important for birdwatchers.

Antpittas and forest-floor birds

Antpittas are a major reason birders talk about Paz de las Aves. These shy forest birds are difficult to find without local experience, making guide-supported birding especially valuable.

Andean Cock-of-the-Rock

The Andean Cock-of-the-Rock is one of the signature birds of northwest Ecuador. Nearby routes can also support broader planning around lek visits, early starts, and realistic photography expectations.

Toucans, tanagers and hummingbirds

Depending on the day and route, birders may look for colorful canopy and edge species such as toucans, tanagers, hummingbirds, woodpeckers and other cloud forest birds.

Mixed flocks and surprises

Cloud forest birding is dynamic. Weather, fruiting trees, elevation, light, and season can influence whether the day feels quiet or suddenly becomes active with mixed-species flocks.

FAQs About Birding Paz de las Aves

Is Paz de las Aves good for beginners?

Yes. With a guide, it can be a very understandable introduction to cloud forest birding because the experience is focused and easier to interpret.

What birds can I see there?

Possible targets include antpittas, Andean Cock-of-the-Rock, toucans, tanagers, hummingbirds, woodpeckers and other cloud forest birds.

Can it be part of a Mindo tour?

Yes. It can be planned as part of a private birding day or paired with other Mindo routes depending on travel time and your target species.

Is it good for photographers?

It can be rewarding for photography because the pace is often more focused than a general hike, but sightings and conditions always vary.
